Pho Bo

Description

Pho Bo is a Vietnamese noodle soup renowned for its aromatic broth and rice noodles. The broth is crafted with a harmonious blend of bone marrow, spices like star anise and cinnamon, and charred onions and ginger. Served piping hot, the soup is adorned with thin slices of meat, fresh herbs like basil and cilantro, bean sprouts, and lime wedges. Pho embodies a perfect balance of savory, sweet, and herbaceous flavors, creating a comforting and aromatic culinary experience that is a staple of Vietnamese cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Beef bones (marrow and knuckle bones)
  • Beef brisket or flank
  • Rice noodles (banh pho)
  • Onions (charred for the broth)
  • Ginger (charred for the broth)
  • Star anise
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Whole cloves
  • Coriander seeds
  • Fish sauce
  • Rock sugar or granulated sugar
  • Salt
  • Bean sprouts
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Lime wedges

Steps

  1. Char onions and ginger over an open flame or under a broiler until they are slightly blackened
  2. In a large pot, add beef bones, brisket or flank, charred onions, charred ginger, star anise, cinnamon sticks, cloves, and coriander seeds
  3. Fill the pot with water (about 4-5 quarts) and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for at least 1.5 to 2 hours, skimming impurities from the surface
  4. Add fish sauce, rock sugar or granulated sugar, and salt to the broth. Adjust the seasoning to taste
  5. Cook rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside
  6. Thinly slice beef brisket or flank
  7. Wash and prepare bean sprouts, basil leaves, cilantro leaves, lime wedges, and Thai bird chilies
  8. Arrange a portion of rice noodles in serving bowls
  9. Top with sliced raw beef
  10. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and beef. The hot broth will cook the raw beef slices
  11. Serve the Pho hot, adding bean sprouts, basil, cilantro, lime wedges, and chilies to liking
