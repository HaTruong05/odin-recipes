Okonomiyaki

Description

Okonomiyaki is a Japanese savory pancake that blends a flavorful batter of flour, dashi, and eggs with shredded cabbage, green onions, and a choice of protein like pork or shrimp. Cooked on a griddle until golden and crispy, the pancake is then brushed with a savory-sweet okonomiyaki sauce, topped with mayonnaise, and garnished with aonori and katsuobushi. The result is a delicious and customizable dish that effortlessly combines textures and tastes, offering a delightful culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups of dashi (Japanese soup)
  • 3 eggs
  • 4 cups of shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup of sliced green onions
  • 1 cup diced cooked pork or shrimp (optional)
  • Okonomiyaki sauce (a mixture of Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, ketchup, and sugar)
  • Mayonnaise
  • Aonori (dried seaweed flakes)
  • Katsuobushi (bonito flakes)
  • Vegetable oil

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 ½ cups of dashi, and 3 beaten eggs until smooth
  2. Mix in 4 cups of shredded cabbage, 1 cup of sliced green onions, and 1 cup of diced cooked pork or shrimp
  3. Preheat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat
  4. Ladle the batter onto the griddle, forming a round pancake. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy
  5. Brush the top of the pancake with Okonomiyaki sauce
  6. Drizzle mayonnaise over the pancake and sprinkle with aonori and katsuobushi
  7. Once cooked and topped, serve the Okonomiyaki immediately while it's hot
